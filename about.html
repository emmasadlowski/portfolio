About Emma


Throughout my life, I have always been fascinated by technology and the process of making complex topics more accessible and easily understandable.


My Experience

I've always had a deep love for learning that translates in my day-to-day work and creative passions. I enjoy the challenge of quickly becoming familiar with a new subject, and using what I know to craft compelling stories and content.

While studying Human Development and Sociology at UC Davis, I worked as a science and tech journalist for my university's student newspaper, where I wrote weekly stories about science and tech in the community. I interviewed some of the brightest professors, scientists, and healthcare professionals at UC Davis, and transformed their thoughts and ideas into unique stories for our readers.

I taught in an elementary school after graduatiion, but pivoted back into science and tech writing in 2019 to take on a Content Strategist role with The Bulleit Group, an award-winning public relations agency. While there, I worked with our account teams to transform our clients’ business goals into strategic and memorable content output. My role involved drafting and editing press releases, blog posts, internal messaging docs, social media posts, presentation decks, newsletters, internal documentation, and more. I also worked on long-term content projects with my team, such as competitive audits and full-scale brand overhauls, to outline strategic recommendations for optimal content strategies.

Now, I'm ready for my next challenge and am diving deeper into the technical realm. I'm currently pursuing a career in technical writing, information architecture, API documentation, and/or content strategy.


At my core, I am a curious, organized, and reliable team player. With every project I take on, I ensure that my team has all the resources we need to successfully take the project through its lifecycle and meet our goals. My curiosity has only grown as I dive deeper into my technical communications practice — I would love more than anything to continue learning in my work and in my professional development. I am motivated to help others understand complex topics and make learning experiences enjoyable (and accessible) for any audience. Aside from my writing and project management skills, I am also proficient in HTML/CSS, Git, and DITA XML.
