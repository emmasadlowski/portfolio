About Emma


Throughout my life, I have always been fascinated by technology and the process of making complex topics more accessible and easily understandable.


My Story

Like many kids in the mid-2000s, I suppose you could say I was obsessed with the online virtual pet world that was neopets.com. At one point, I came across an HTML tutorial on the website that taught users how to customize their own Neopet webpages (who knew you could do that?). Excited by my discovery, I spent hours tinkering with the front-end interface, determined to figure out how to display the HTML/CSS styles I liked and show off my creative flair. I felt empowered as a kid to have easy-to-follow user guides and tutorials that taught me how to develop webpages.

At the same time, I had so many questions about how it all actually worked behind the scenes.

That curiosity followed me into high school and university. Years later, I took up a job as a science and tech journalist for my university's student newspaper, where I wrote weekly stories about science and tech in the UC Davis community. I interviewed some of the brightest professors, scientists, and healthcare professionals at UC Davis, and transformed their thoughts and ideas into unique stories for our readers. It was an experience that I'm forever grateful for, and one that set the foundation for my professional career.

After a year of teaching, I pivoted back into science and tech writing in 2019 to take on a Content Strategist role with The Bulleit Group, an award-winning public relations agency. While there, I worked with our account teams to transform our clients’ business goals into strategic and memorable content output. My role involved drafting and editing press releases, blog posts, internal messaging docs, social media posts, presentation decks, newsletters, internal documentation, and more. I also worked on long-term content projects with my team, such as competitive audits and full-scale brand overhauls, to outline strategic recommendations for optimal content strategies.

Now, I'm ready for my next challenge and am diving deeper into the technical realm. I'm currently pursuing a career in technical writing, information architecture, API documentation, and/or content strategy.


At my core, I am a curious, organized, and reliable team player. With every project I take on, I ensure that my team has all the resources we need to successfully take the project through its lifecycle and meet our goals. While I no longer dabble in creating silly webpages for my virtual pets, my curiosity has only grown as I dive deeper into my technical communications practice. I want to continue learning in my work and in my professional development. I am motivated to help others understand complex topics and make learning experiences enjoyable (and accessible) for any audience. Aside from my writing and project management skills, I am also proficient in HTML/CSS, Git, and DITA XML.
