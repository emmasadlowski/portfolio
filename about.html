About Emma


Throughout my life, I have always been fascinated by technology, as well as how to make complex topics more accessible and easily understandable.


My Story

It all started when I was 11 years old, and like many kids my age, I was obsessed with the online virtual pet world called neopets.com. At one point, I came across an HTML tutorial on the website that taught users how to customize their own webpages! Who knew you could do that? I was enthralled by the idea of customizing my Neopet's own webpage using their online (albeit clunky) lessons and resources. I spent hours tinkering with the front-end interface and learning how to type — all while becoming more familiar with how computers worked. At the same time, I had so many questions about how it all actually worked. I wanted to know more.

Many years later, I took up a job as a science and tech journalist for my university's student newspaper, where I wrote weekly stories about science and tech in the UC Davis community. I had the pleasure of interviewing some of the brightest professors, scientists, and healthcare professionals at UC Davis — taking their thoughts and ideas and transforming them into stories for the UC Davis student body. It was an experience that I'm forever grateful for, and one that set the foundation for my professional career.

I took my love for learning into the classroom after graduating UC Davis in 2018. As an elementary assistant teacher, I specialized in teaching language arts classes and providing one-on-one writing assistance.

Soon after, I pivoted back into science and technology writing to take on a Content Strategist role with The Bulleit Group, an award-winning public relations agency. While there, I worked with our account teams to transform our clients’ business goals into strategic, memorable, and error-free content output. My role involved drafting press releases, blog posts, internal messaging docs, social media posts, presentation decks, newsletters, internal documentation, and more — all within a fast-paced, deadline-driven working environment. I have also worked on long-term content projects, such as competitive audits and entire brand overhauls, which have helped our clients to adopt more optimal content strategies. My editorial contributions to The Bulleit Group have helped our clients win awards and become strategic, competitive forces in their respective industries.

Now, I'm ready for my next challenge and diving deeper into the technical realm. I'm currently pursuing a career in technical writing, information architecture, API documentation, and/or content strategy.


At my core, I am a curious, organized, and reliable team player. With every project I take on, I ensure that our team has all the resources we need to successfully take the project through its lifecycle and meet our goals. While I no longer dabble in creating silly webpages for my virtual pets, my curiousity has only grown as I dive deeper into the technical space. I am motivated to help others understand complex topics and make learning experiences enjoyable (and accessible) for any audience. Aside from my writing and project management skills, I am also proficient in HTML/CSS, Git, and DITA XML.

When I'm not writing, I love hiking in the North San Diego County area, working on creative projects, and trying out new coffee shops. Right now I'm learning JavaScript and Adobe InDesign!
